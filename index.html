The Challenge of Measurement

How do you measure something abstract like "happiness," "intelligence," or "customer satisfaction"? This is where operational definitions come in!

Key Definitions:

  • Concept: Abstract idea or phenomenon (e.g., stress, motivation, quality)
  • Variable: A characteristic that can vary or change (measurable)
  • Operational Definition: Specific, measurable way to define a concept
  • Indicator: Observable measure that represents the concept

üìä Classification of Variables

Variables Come in Many Forms

Understanding variable types helps you choose the right analysis methods and measurement approaches.

By Role in Research:

Independent Variables (IV)

The cause or predictor

Examples: Treatment type, education level, age group

Dependent Variables (DV)

The effect or outcome

Examples: Test scores, blood pressure, sales revenue

Other Types:

  • Moderating: Affects the relationship between IV and DV
  • Mediating: Explains how IV affects DV
  • Control: Held constant to isolate other effects
  • Confounding: Unwanted variables that affect results

By Nature of Data:

Quantitative Variables

  • Discrete: Countable (number of children, cars sold)
  • Continuous: Measurable on a continuum (height, temperature)

Qualitative Variables

  • Nominal: Categories with no order (color, gender)
  • Ordinal: Categories with order (ratings, rankings)
